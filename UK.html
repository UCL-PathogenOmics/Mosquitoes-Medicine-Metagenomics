Course Programme - UK Clinical, Laboratory and Bioinformatics

Day 1 — Mosquito-Borne Diseases
  • Welcome & overview
  • Malaria I: epidemiology, transmission dynamics and clinical presentation
  • Malaria II: vaccines and treatment – the old and the new
  • Malaria III: is eradication possible?
  • Dengue I: virology, epidemiology and pathophysiology
  • Dengue II: management and prevention – present and future vaccines
  • Chikungunya and Zika
Day 2 — Mosquito-Borne Diseases & Laboratory Work (Proteomics)
  • Yellow fever
  • West Nile virus
  • Japanese encephalitis
  • Emerging mosquito pathogens
  • Case studies: malaria, dengue and Zika
  • Lab safety & biosecurity
  • Laboratory session: preparing, running and interpreting MALDI
Day 3 — Laboratory Work: DNA/RNA Extraction & Sequencing
  • DNA/RNA extraction
  • Quality control (concentration, fragment length and purity)
  • Nanopore sequencing
Day 4 — Bioinformatics
  • Introduction to sequencing files and quality control
  • DNA genome assembly
  • Phylogenetic reconstruction (Xavier Didelot)
  • RNA transcriptomics
Day 5 — Bioinformatics Workshop, Surveillance & Vector Control
  • Bioinformatics workshop: interpreting sequencing results
  • Vector control tools (IRS, larviciding, Wolbachia, sterile males)
  • Community engagement for vector control
  • Climate change and mosquito-borne diseases: current trends and future projections
  • Review of mosquito photography
  • Wrap-up & course evaluation
